About The Author

My name is Skyler Adams, and I strongly believe that passion is the key to finding a fulfilling career. After graduating from high school in 2020, I started my college journey with a major in business, hoping to find my calling in the corporate world. However, it quickly became apparent to me that this path wasn't quite right for me, and I found myself struggling to stay motivated.

Determined to find my true passion, I made the difficult decision to switch my major to computer science, hoping that it would spark my interest. While I did enjoy aspects of the program, I still felt unfulfilled and unsure of what the future held for me.

That's when I discovered MAX technical training, and everything changed. I immediately fell in love with coding and found myself energized by the prospect of pursuing a career in software development. I bet on myself and my work ethic, and made the brave decision to quit my job to pursue full-time enrollment at MAX.

Through my time at MAX, I found myself amongst a community of like-minded individuals who shared my passion for coding. The curriculum was challenging, and I often found myself working tirelessly to stay on top of the coursework. However, my passion for the subject kept me motivated, and I was determined to succeed.

Despite the long hours and hard work, I found that my persistence paid off. I discovered my true calling as a software developer, and I am excited to start my career in the tech industry. I am grateful for the opportunities that MAX has given me, and I am confident in my skills and knowledge to succeed in this rapidly evolving field.

For me, settling for anything less than a career that fulfills me and allows me to make a positive impact on the world is not an option. With my unwavering passion, dedication, and work ethic, I know that I am on the right path towards a fulfilling and successful career in software development.

Why You Should Consider a Bootcamp

  • This could help you to advance your career or make a whole new career path for you to follow
  • You will learn a lot and get a lot of value out of it
  • There are many bootcamps out there so you can pick and choose what field/industry you want to be in

Why You Shouldn't Consider a Bootcamp

  • The course will be fast paced if you're not ready to move fast then this may not be for you
  • If you can only attend online but learn better in an inperson environment
